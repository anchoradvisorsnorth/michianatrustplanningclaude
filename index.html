Education First

What a revocable living trust actually does

A trust is simply a legal container that holds your assets during your life — and transfers them to your loved ones after you're gone, without the cost and delay of probate court.

❌ Without a Trust

  • Your estate goes through probate court — a public, expensive, slow process
  • Can take 12–24 months before your family receives anything
  • Attorney and court fees can consume 3–7% of your estate
  • Your will becomes public record — anyone can see it
  • A judge decides who gets what if documents are unclear
  • Family members may challenge the distribution

✅ With a Revocable Living Trust

  • Assets transfer directly to beneficiaries — no court, no delay
  • Settlement often complete within weeks, not years
  • Minimal cost — no probate attorney needed at death
  • Completely private — never becomes public record
  • You stay in full control during your lifetime
  • You can change, amend, or revoke it anytime
