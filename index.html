Education First

What a revocable living trust actually does

A trust is a legal management structure — not just a document. It controls how your assets are managed during your life, during incapacity, and after death. When funded properly, it keeps your family out of probate court.

❌ Without a Trust

  • ✗Estate goes through Indiana probate court — public, slow, and expensive
  • ✗Can take 12–24 months before family receives anything
  • ✗Probate fees and court costs can consume 3–7% of your estate
  • ✗Your will becomes public record — anyone can read it
  • ✗A judge may decide how assets are distributed if documents conflict
  • ✗Incapacity can trigger guardianship proceedings, which are costly and public

✅ With a Funded Revocable Living Trust

  • ✓Assets transfer directly to beneficiaries — no court required
  • ✓Settlement often complete in weeks, not months
  • ✓Completely private — never becomes public record
  • ✓You stay in full control during your lifetime
  • ✓Successor trustee steps in without court involvement if you're incapacitated
  • ✓You can amend, update, or revoke the trust at any time

What You Receive

Your Indiana estate plan includes documents that work together as a system. Each one has a specific role.

📜

Revocable Living Trust

The main management document. Controls asset management during your lifetime, during incapacity, and at death. Avoids probate when funded.

📋

Pour-Over Will

The safety net. If an asset is outside the trust at death, this will directs it into the trust. Also nominates guardians for minor children.

🏦

Certificate of Trust

A short verification document for banks and institutions. Confirms your trust and trustee authority without disclosing private distribution terms.

✍️

Durable Financial POA

Appoints an agent to handle your financial matters if you're incapacitated. Includes authority to fund or amend your trust under Indiana Code 30-5.

🏥

Health Care Rep + Living Will

Covers medical decisions and end-of-life instructions. Ensures your healthcare preferences are followed if you can't communicate them.

🛋️

Assignment of Personal Property

Transfers your household goods, furniture, jewelry, collectibles, and other tangible personal property into the trust.

🏠

Quitclaim Deed to Trust

Retitles your Indiana real estate into the trust. If property is not retitled, it may still require probate — this document prevents that.

Signing is only half the job. Funding makes it work.

Most online services hand you documents and walk away. We don't. An unfunded trust provides none of the probate-avoidance benefits you're paying for. That's why we include a complete post-signing funding guide.

  • 3Transfer real estate using the Quitclaim Deed — recorded with your county
  • 4Retitle bank and brokerage accounts using your Certificate of Trust
  • 5Review beneficiary designations on retirement accounts and life insurance
  • 6Confirm personal property and business interests assigned to trust
  • 7Build a funding proof file with copies of all confirmations
  • 8Schedule annual maintenance reviews

Common Funding Mistakes to Avoid

  • Assuming signing alone avoids probate — it doesn't
  • Forgetting to record real estate deeds with your Indiana county
  • Leaving newly opened accounts outside trust ownership
  • Failing to update beneficiary designations on retirement accounts after life changes
  • Not keeping documentation of completed transfers
  • Creating the trust but never moving assets into it

Yes. Indiana does not require an attorney to create a revocable living trust. Indiana law permits individuals to prepare and execute their own estate planning documents. Our templates were drafted and reviewed by a licensed Indiana estate attorney to comply with Indiana law, including the Indiana Trust Code (IC 30-4) and related statutes.
A will only takes effect after death and must go through Indiana probate court — a public, slow, and often expensive process. A revocable living trust takes effect immediately, allows your assets to transfer privately and directly to beneficiaries without court involvement, and lets you stay in complete control during your lifetime. Most Indiana estate planning attorneys recommend a trust-based plan over a will-only plan for families with any significant assets or real estate.
Funding means transferring ownership of your assets into the trust — retitling your home, bank accounts, and investment accounts so the trust owns them. A signed trust that holds no assets provides none of the probate-avoidance benefits. This is the number one mistake families make. Every Michiana Trust Planning bundle includes a complete Indiana Asset Funding Guide with step-by-step instructions for each type of asset.
Yes. To transfer Indiana real estate into your trust, the Quitclaim Deed must be recorded with the recorder's office in the county where the property is located. Recording requirements vary by Indiana county. Your funding guide includes instructions for this step. The transfer is exempt from Indiana transfer tax because it's a transfer to a revocable trust in which you retain a beneficial interest (Indiana Code 6-2.1-3-3).
